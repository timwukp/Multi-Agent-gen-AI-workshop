Amazon Q1 2025 Earnings Report

SEATTLE, WA - Amazon.com, Inc. (NASDAQ: AMZN) today announced financial results for its first quarter ended March 31, 2025.

Key Financial Highlights:
- Net sales increased 12% to $143.3 billion in the first quarter
- Operating income was $15.3 billion in the first quarter
- Net income was $10.4 billion, or $9.94 per diluted share
- AWS net sales were $25.0 billion, up 17% year-over-year

Business Segment Performance:
- North America segment sales: $82.5 billion (up 8%)
- International segment sales: $31.9 billion (up 10%)
- AWS segment sales: $25.0 billion (up 17%)

"We're pleased with our strong start to 2025," said Andy Jassy, Amazon CEO. "Our continued focus on customer obsession and long-term thinking is driving growth across all our business segments."

Cash Flow and Balance Sheet:
- Operating cash flow increased to $84.9 billion for the trailing twelve months
- Free cash flow increased to $35.5 billion for the trailing twelve months
- Cash and marketable securities totaled $73.4 billion

Forward-Looking Guidance:
For Q2 2025, Amazon expects net sales between $144.0 billion and $149.0 billion, representing growth of 7% to 11% compared to Q2 2024.
